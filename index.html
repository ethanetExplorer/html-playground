The Singapore MRT system

The MRT system in Singapore is a rapid transit system that serves the city-state of Singapore. It was opened in 1987 and currently consists of 7 lines with a total of 202 stations, covering a distance of 230.5 km. The system is operated by two companies, SMRT Corporation and SBS Transit, and it is known for its efficiency, cleanliness, and punctuality. The MRT system is a popular mode of transportation for both locals and tourists, and it is often seen as a symbol of Singapore's modern and efficient infrastructure.

North-South Line

This is the first MRT line that was opened in 1987 and runs from Jurong East in the west to Marina South Pier in the south. It is 44.7 km long and has 28 stations. The NSL is one of the busiest lines in the system, serving many important destinations such as Orchard Road, Raffles Place, and Marina Bay.

East-West Line

The EWL is the second MRT line that was opened in 1988 and runs from Pasir Ris in the east to Tuas Link in the west. It is 57.2 km long and has 35 stations. The EWL is the longest line in the system and is also one of the busiest, serving important destinations such as Changi Airport, Jurong East, and City Hall.

North-East Line

The NEL was opened in 2003 and runs from HarbourFront in the south to Punggol in the northeast. It is 20 km long and has 16 stations. The NEL serves many important destinations such as Clarke Quay, Chinatown, and Serangoon.

Circle Line

The CCL is a circular line that was opened in stages between 2009 and 2011. It runs from HarbourFront in the south to Dhoby Ghaut in the north, and then back down to Marina Bay in the south. It is 35.5 km long and has 33 stations. The CCL serves many important destinations such as Botanic Gardens, Esplanade, and Paya Lebar.

Downtown Line

The DTL was opened in stages between 2013 and 2017 and runs from Bukit Panjang in the northwest to Expo in the east. It is 42.2 km long and has 34 stations. The DTL serves many important destinations such as Bukit Timah, Little India, and Bugis.

Thomson-East Coast Line

The TEL is a new line that is currently under construction and is expected to be fully operational by 2024. It will run from Woodlands in the north to Tanjong Katong in the east, and will eventually be extended further south to Sungei Bedok. It is 43 km long and will have 31 stations. The TEL will serve many important destinations such as Woodlands Regional Centre, Marina Bay, and Orchard.

Jurong Region Line

The JRL is another new line that is currently under construction and is expected to be fully operational by 2028. It will serve the western part of Singapore, connecting important areas such as Jurong East, Tengah, and Jurong Industrial Estate. The JRL will be 24 km long and will have 24 stations.

LRTs

Bukit Panjang LRT

The Bukit Panjang LRT is a light rail transit system that serves the Bukit Panjang area in the northwestern part of Singapore. It was opened in 1999 and is connected to the Bukit Panjang MRT station on the Downtown Line. The Bukit Panjang LRT is 7.8 km long and has 14 stations. It is the only LRT system in the MRT network and is known for its scenic views of the surrounding greenery. The Bukit Panjang LRT is also an important mode of transportation for residents in the area, connecting them to important destinations such as Bukit Panjang Plaza and Choa Chu Kang.

Sengkang and Punggol LRTs

The Sengkang and Punggol LRTs is a light rail transit system that serves the Sengkang and Punggol areas in the northeastern part of Singapore. It was opened in 2003 and is connected to the Sengkang and Punggol MRT stations on the North-East Line. The Sengkang-Punggol LRT is 11.1 km long and has 14 stations. It is known for its elevated tracks that provide scenic views of the surrounding neighborhoods. The Sengkang-Punggol LRT is an important mode of transportation for residents in the area, connecting them to important destinations such as Sengkang General Hospital and Waterway Point.
